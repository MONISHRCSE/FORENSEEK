III. ESTIMATED TIME OF DEATH (TOD):
Based on rigor mortis, algor mortis (body temperature), and lividity observed at the scene, the estimated time of death is between 10:15 PM and 10:45 PM on May 9, 2026. This timeline aligns with the observed state of gastric emptying.

IV. EXTERNAL EXAMINATION:
The body is that of a well-developed, well-nourished adult male. 
- Gunshot Wound: A single entrance wound is located on the left anterior chest, 4 inches left of the midline. No exit wound is present. Stippling is present around the entrance wound, indicating a close-range discharge (estimated 2 to 4 feet).
- Head Trauma: A massive stellate laceration (6 cm) is present on the back of the head (occipital region), associated with an underlying depressed skull fracture. This injury is consistent with being struck from behind with a heavy, blunt object, such as a metal pipe.
- Defensive Wounds: Distinct contusions and abrasions are present on the bilateral forearms (ulnar aspect). The fingernails of the right hand are broken, and epidermal tissue was recovered from beneath the nail beds, highly indicative of a physical struggle prior to incapacitation.

V. INTERNAL EXAMINATION:
- Cardiovascular System: The projectile perforated the left ventricle of the heart and lodged in the descending aorta. Massive hemothorax is noted.
- Respiratory System: Lungs are unremarkable except for left lower lobe collapse due to blood accumulation.
- Nervous System: Subdural hematoma corresponding to the external occipital trauma.

VI. TOXICOLOGY:
Blood and vitreous fluid were submitted for toxicological screening.
- Blood Alcohol Concentration (BAC): 0.04 g/dL.
- Narcotics/Drugs of Abuse: Negative for opiates, amphetamines, cocaine, and barbiturates.
- No evidence of poisoning or toxic substance ingestion.

VII. SUMMARY AND OPINION:
It is my medical opinion that the decedent died as a result of a penetrating gunshot wound to the chest, compounded by severe blunt force trauma to the head. The presence of defensive wounds on the forearms suggests the victim was involved in a physical altercation before the fatal injuries were inflicted. The close-range nature of the gunshot wound indicates the shooter was in close proximity. Death was rapid.
